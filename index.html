A Brief Summary of Colorado 14ers

Colorado's Highest Peaks

Long's Peak

The lone 14er in Colorado's Rocky Mountain National Park, Longs Peak is one of the state's most popular hiking spots. After it's first ascent in 1868 by John Wesley Powell, 15,000 hikers and climbers attempt to summit it each year. About half will see the views from the top. Partly due to its traffic and partly due to its difficult it has earned the dubious reputation of "Colorado's Deadliest Mountain"

By far the most popular route to climb this beast is via the "Keyhole Route". This route brings it's victims up a deceptively easy 7.4 mile hike to the Boulderfields, a barren wasteland of boulders sitting just below the summit. This is a popular spot for overnight campers. After the Boulderfields climbers will traverse through the routes iconic keyhole and along narrow exposed routes, ending in a 300 foot Class 3 scramble to the summit.

This hike is not for the faint of heart. Hikers die summiting Long's Peak each year. If you plan to attempt it, be prepared for a lot of exposure and technical hiking, potentially requiring crampons and some serious climbing skills.
